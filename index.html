I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Harvard & Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics. I am working on advanced millimetre- and submillimetre-wave receivers for radio astronomy. Among other applications, these receivers help us to study star formation, both in our galaxy and across cosmic time.

The receivers that I work on are based on Superconductor-Insulator-Superconductor (SIS) tunnel junctions. These devices are so sensitive that the noise properties are now only a few times the quantum limit that is imposed by the Heisenberg uncertainty principle.
